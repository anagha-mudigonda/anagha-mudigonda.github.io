The Story

YuppTV is an internet TV viewing platform for the Indian audience, Yupptv is visited by more than 1 million users per month (Yupptv.com Traffic Statistics, 2018.), across 11+ countries. YuppTV allows access through 25 devices including desktop, mobile and tablet devices


Research Question

How might we create a design system to improve the conversion rates of the Yupptv product?

Outcome

A global design system that is robust and flexible across various platforms improved the product conversions by 11% and reached around 100 thousand subscribers within an year of launch.

OVERVIEW

Research Process

UNDERSTAND THE PROBLEM

Background

To understand the pain points of customers while using YuppTV, we started to investigate the product evolution of YuppTV. After launching its first site in 2007 the website experienced changes to accommodate new product requirements–with several band-aidsolutions, causing various user experience issues. However, we noticed the changes were limited to only website but not for TV and mobile devices. So, we hypothesized there must be a major problem with consistency among the devices.

INSPECT UI

Heuristic Evaluation

We conducted a thorough heuristic evaluation for Mobile, TV, Web devices based on Nelson and Norman heuristic principles. Then, major outcomes are divided into the following categories:

  • Scalability
    Home page, catchup page, the player page looks different in each device.
  • Communication
    The website lacks to communicate about new arrivals, favorite live shows, package expiry information.
  • Discoverability
    Content discoverability (search results, rows display, history) is not consistent across all the devices.


TALK TO EXPERTS

Evidence Through Customer Stories

We did remote user interviews with old and valued customers over email and phone experiences. In this stage, we involved the team to witness first-hand experience with user needs and promote empathy.

Time saving stategry

  • We selected callers from customer care requests, notfied about the research, resulting in a good response rate for feedback calls.
  • Involving stakeholders allowed developers to empathize with customer problems, and this bought a faster buy-in to align our product objectives.




ORGANIZING DATA

Affinity Diagram

With the collected data, I created an affinity diagram to understand how to prioritize the pain points that arose. This time we involved all the stakeholders (business, designers, developers, and marketing) needs and prioritize findings. The critical issues ended up being: content watching, package subscription, onboarding. We brainstormed on the Wishlist that every department desired for product development.

EMPATHIZING WITH CUSTOMERS

Personas

I found two main user groups. One was a stay at home parents with more leisure time to watch movies, Tv shows and working user who prefer to content for family. Creating a persona, pointed to merge the need to create a TV viewing experience for complete family.

LOOKING FOR PAIN POINTS

Customer Journey

Seeing through thee eyes of yupptv user, we identified the touchpoints of the yupptv user with the application, providing opportunity to improve the application experience.

Takeaways

  • Need for more customized content on the home page.
  • Access to recently watched content.
  • Sync the activity across multiple devices.
  • Improve package purchase process efficiency.

EXPERT FEEDBACK

Usability Testing

A remote usability test with a mid-fidelity prototype provided insights into user behavior with the design. Participants were recruited based on the similarity with the YuppTV personas. It is an unmoderated usability test. I devised the study plan focusing on test objectives along with scenarios and tasks for the participants. We used a usability hub platform to conduct the study. In this exercise, I analyzed the qualitative data (open-ended responses) as well as quantitative data (task completion, time on task) to identify usability issues with the designs.

Key findings 

First of all, this test was a success by uncovering usability issues and suprises. Also, the feedback provided by participants was valuable for future iterations.

  • Catchup section placement is required to be in the website first fold. This is because 8/15 participants scrolls down to acess catchup.
  • Text on the LiveTV channel card needs to be outside of the card, this would improve readability
  • It requires further usability test to finalize the theme with real data, since the participants provided mixed responses (9/15 said they would use apps that are both dark as well as white in color).

    • Learnings

    Apart from provding insights into designs, conducting the remote test gave a learning experience for further studies.

  • Better response rate
    Using a yupptv research channel (email) instead of customer care email improved our response rate for follow up questions.
  • Use real images
    Current study contains images designed by graphic designer that are less likely match with real images. This may cause bias. So, we planned to use real images from the video in the future studies.
  • Select a theme color
    It requires more evidence to finalize between black or white, so we planned to release the build version of the application and release in two regions for future study.

    CHECKLIST

    Learnings

    Two years at YuppTV, presented me to work closely with YuppTV customers as well as product teams. It takes an entire product team relentless effort to achieve an accessible product with 100 thousand+ subscribers. Here are a few things I learned as a designer, researcher, and leader.

    • Content creators
      Designing for users is important; however, designing for content uploaders is also required because it saves significant time spent on content uploading.
    • Research, test, and improve
      By doing iterative testing, questions related to user behavior are answered (just like a magic crystal ball).
    • Team objectives
      Learning about different team objectives helped me to collaborate with different teams.
    • Young designers
      Peer-review of designs with young designers, often provided valuable and innovative insights for the product.
    • Data scientists
      Best friends of designers and researchers, working with them uncovers new problem areas to solve in the product.
