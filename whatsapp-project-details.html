The Story

With overwhelming information forwarded in WhatsApp, people mistrust | trust the information without verifying. This may lead to losing trust with the sender or with any vital information.


Research Question

How might we improve reliability| engagement with forward text messages?

Outcome

A hi-fidelity prototype that contains roadblocks to forward text messages.

OVERVIEW

Research Process

UNDERSTAND THE PROBLEM

Background

According to the Lokniti-CSDS Mood of the Nation (MOTN) survey done in 2018, 61% of users mistrust information shared on WhatsApp. Indeed, the lack of trust is due to the large volume of forwarding messages spread without any “control”.

Additionally, I hypothesize that providing friction to send messages allows us to reduce the spread of messages. On the same line, WhatsApp witnessed a 70% drop in 'highly forwarded messages' after imposing limits in April 2020. Thus, it sets a perfect opportunity to test the reliability of information by adding roadblocks to send a message.


Generative survey research

To understand how users perceive WhatsApp messages, I surveyed to know more about their perceptions towards the WhatsApp forward message for 28 current WhatsApp users Survey results.


QUANTIFYING IMPORTANCE

Connect 28 responses

After the first round of analysis, I found that the multiple user groups are interested to read, verify the information in the message. Also, based on the sender, message received type and user age range. For example:

  • Sender type
    Stranger, Friend, Family, Authentic sources.
  • Message received type
    Stranger, Friend, Family, Authentic sources.
  • Occupation | age
    Students (18-24), working professional (25-42), entrepreneurs (42- 65).
  • Task type
     The reader would either like read and verify the message (examination model), ignore the message (neglect model) and provide an opinion (impression model).

From this exploration, I observed a few patterns:

  • Messages from the group often get unnoticed.
  • The genuine message gets buried.
  • A personal forward message may have a possibility to read and verify.
  • Check message facts in the current design is time consuming.

RAPID ITERATIONS

Low Fidelity

Given our research findings, I came up with three design ideas to address the insights. Educating users about the summary feature, verify feature, and mark the message useful.




User Feedback

Usability Test Overview

It is a remote moderated usability test, post-test method, and with-in subjects study for current X new design.



Study Procedure

  • To avoid order effects and promote consistent learning
    Each participant is exposed to both current and new design in same order .
  • Think-out loud protocol to understand mental models
    Participants are instructed to think-out loud method along with answering cognitive probes.
  • Post test
    Post session participant completes a post-task questionnaire , post-test questionnaire (SUPR-Q) , product desirability questionnaire for current and new design.

CHECKLIST

Learnings

This was my project as a part of UX Research program UCB Extension. It was a great learning experience with Jen who taught us ways to plan and conduct research in the remote settings. These are the top 3 things I can reflect upon:

  • Test with enough users
    Data from five participants(n=5) is hard to define statistical significance, it is important to test with more users. Plan to recruit more participants to avoid no-shows.
  • Recruit diverse user groups
    Including age groups above 55 provide a varied data could be generalized for a scalable audience.
  • Conduct pilot study
    conducting a dry run of the while procedure helped to identity gaps in the procedure and other material.
  • Transcribe data
    data analysis is laborious process without having a clear objective, It took 2 days for me to understand the data and prioritize..
